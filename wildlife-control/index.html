Responsible and Accountable Our Detailed Wildlife Control Process

Effective wildlife control requires a systematic and thorough approach. At Broomfield Pest Pros, our process is designed to be comprehensive, ensuring that we not only remove the present nuisance but also implement measures to prevent future problems. Our detailed process includes:

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first step in our wildlife control service in Broomfield, CO, is a detailed inspection of your property. Our experienced technicians will carefully examine your home or business, both inside and out, to identify signs of wildlife presence, including droppings, tracks, gnaw marks, nesting materials, and potential entry points. Identifying the Intruder

Different animals leave different clues. During the inspection, we look for specific indicators that help us identify the species of wildlife causing the issue. This might involve examining the size and shape of droppings, the type of damage to insulation or wood, and the characteristics of nesting materials. Knowing the specific animal is vital for choosing the most effective and humane removal strategy.

Mapping Entry Points

Wildlife often gains access to structures through various openings, some of which may not be obvious to the untrained eye. We meticulously search for potential entry points such as gaps in siding or roofing, damaged soffits, open vents, uncapped chimneys, and foundation cracks. Identifying and sealing these entry points is a critical part of preventing future infestations.

Development of a Customized Removal Plan

Once we have completed the inspection and identified the animal and its entry points, we develop a customized removal plan tailored to your specific situation. Selecting Humane Removal Methods

Our priority is always to use the most humane removal methods possible while ensuring the safety of both the animals and the inhabitants of your property. This may involve live trapping and relocation to a suitable natural habitat away from residential areas, hazing techniques to encourage animals to leave voluntarily, or other non-lethal methods as appropriate. We strictly adhere to local wildlife regulations and guidelines during the removal process.

Implementing Exclusion Strategies

Removing the animal is only half the battle. To prevent future problems, we implement exclusion strategies to seal off all identified entry points. This may involve repairing damaged structures, installing wildlife-resistant screening over vents and chimneys, and reinforcing vulnerable areas. Responsible and Accountable Common Wildlife Problems in Broomfield, CO and How We Solve Them

Living in Broomfield, CO offers residents a beautiful connection to nature, but this proximity also means that encounters with wildlife are a common occurrence. While many animals are simply passing through, some can become problematic when they start seeking shelter or food within human structures. Understanding the signs of these issues is the first step in addressing them effectively. If you're noticing unusual activity or damage around your property, it might be time to consider professional wildlife control in Broomfield, CO. Our team at Broomfield Pest Pros is well-versed in handling the specific types of wildlife that are prevalent in this area and can offer lasting solutions. We also offer exceptional rodent control services if that's more of your issue.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

The pitter-patter of little feet, scratching, or even chattering sounds coming from your attic or within your walls, particularly during the night or early morning, are strong indicators of a wildlife presence. These sounds can be unsettling and disruptive to your sleep and overall peace of mind. Common culprits in Broomfield might include squirrels, raccoons, mice, or even birds. These animals seek the warmth and shelter of your home, especially during colder months or breeding season.

Why it Matters:

Beyond the annoying noise, animals in your attic or walls can cause significant damage. They might chew on electrical wires, creating a serious fire hazard. They can also tear up insulation for nesting materials, reducing your home's energy efficiency and increasing heating and cooling costs. Their droppings and urine can accumulate, leading to unpleasant odors and potential health risks due to bacteria and parasites. Addressing these noises promptly with professional wildlife control can prevent costly repairs and health issues.

Damaged Wiring or Ductwork

Finding evidence of chewed wires, damaged insulation, or torn air ducts is a clear sign that an animal has been active within your home's infrastructure. Animals, particularly rodents and squirrels, have a natural instinct to gnaw to wear down their constantly growing teeth. Your home's wiring and ductwork provide convenient and often hidden targets.

Why it Matters:

Damaged electrical wiring poses a severe fire risk, potentially leading to short circuits and dangerous situations. Compromised ductwork can reduce the efficiency of your heating and cooling systems, leading to higher energy bills and uneven temperatures throughout your home. Furthermore, damaged insulation reduces your home's ability to regulate temperature, impacting comfort and energy consumption. Our squirrel removal services and other wildlife control methods address the animal causing the damage and can help you assess and repair the damage they've inflicted. We don't just remove the animal; we help you restore your home's integrity.

Unpleasant Odors

Persistent and unexplained foul odors within your home, particularly in areas like the attic, crawl space, or walls, can be a telltale sign of a dead animal or accumulated animal waste. Decomposing animals or large amounts of urine and feces create strong, offensive smells that can permeate your entire home, making it uncomfortable to live in.

Why it Matters:

Beyond the unpleasantness, these odors can attract insects and other pests, exacerbating the problem. More importantly, animal waste can contain harmful bacteria, viruses, and parasites that pose health risks to humans and pets. Inhaling airborne particles from dried droppings can lead to respiratory issues and other illnesses. Our wildlife control services include locating and safely removing deceased animals or cleaning up accumulated waste, eliminating the source of the odor and mitigating health risks.

Visible Entry Points or structural Damage

Noticing holes or gaps in your roofline, soffits, siding, foundation, or chimney are clear indicators that wildlife has found a way into your property. Animals are resourceful and can exploit even small openings to gain entry. You might also see evidence of gnawing around these openings or bent and damaged vents or screens.

Why it Matters:

Visible entry points mean your home is vulnerable to continued wildlife intrusion. What starts as one animal can quickly become a family, leading to larger infestations and more significant damage. Furthermore, these openings can allow moisture, insects, and other pests to enter your home, creating additional problems. Our wildlife control service in Broomfield, CO involves identifying these entry points and implementing exclusion measures to seal them off permanently, preventing future access and protecting your home from the elements and other potential invaders. This is a crucial step in long-term residential protection.

Unusual Animal Activity Around Your Property

Observing animals repeatedly entering or exiting a specific area of your home or spending an unusual amount of time near your foundation, roof, or outbuildings can be a sign that they are attempting to gain entry or have already established a presence. You might see raccoons rummaging through your trash cans consistently, squirrels attempting to climb walls to reach your attic, or skunks digging under your porch.

Why it Matters:

Increased animal activity around your property indicates that your home is a potential target. Animals are often attracted to properties that offer easy access to food, water, and shelter. Addressing this behavior early with professional wildlife control intervention can prevent them from successfully entering your home and establishing an infestation. We can assess what is attracting the animals and offer solutions, such as securing trash cans or advising on landscaping changes, in addition to our removal and exclusion services. Our raccoon removal services are particularly sought after when these clever creatures become a nuisance.

Damage to Gardens or Landscaping

Finding your vegetable garden raided, flower beds dug up, or trees and shrubs damaged can be the work of various wildlife, such as rabbits, deer, or groundhogs. While not directly an issue within your home's structure, this type of damage can be frustrating and costly for homeowners who invest time and resources in their landscaping.

Why it Matters:

Damage to gardens and landscaping not only diminishes the aesthetic appeal of your property but can also destroy valuable plants and crops. Some animals can even damage the roots of trees or burrow tunnels that can undermine structures. While our primary focus is often on structural infestations for wildlife control in Broomfield, CO, we can offer advice and strategies for deterring wildlife from your outdoor spaces as part of a comprehensive approach to managing wildlife encounters on your property.

Pet Behavior Changes

If your pets are displaying unusual behavior, such as barking incessantly at specific areas of your home, scratching at walls, or showing signs of anxiety or fear in certain rooms, it could indicate the presence of wildlife within your home. Pets often have a heightened sense of smell and hearing and can detect animals that you may not be aware of.

Why it Matters:

Your pet's behavior can be an early warning sign of a potential wildlife problem. Ignoring these cues could allow an infestation to worsen unnoticed. Pay attention to where your pets are focusing their attention and investigate those areas for signs of wildlife. If you suspect wildlife is the cause of your pet's anxiety, contacting a professional wildlife control service is the next logical step to confirm and address the issue.

Droppings or Urine Stains

Discovering animal droppings in or around your home, or noticing yellow or brown stains accompanied by a strong odor, are undeniable signs of a wildlife presence. The size, shape, and location of the droppings can help identify the type of animal responsible.

Why it Matters:

Animal droppings and urine are unsanitary and can contain a variety of harmful pathogens that can cause diseases in humans and pets. The odors they produce can also be persistent and difficult to eliminate without professional cleaning. Furthermore, the presence of droppings indicates that animals are not just passing through but are likely using your property as a latrine or even nesting area, requiring immediate wildlife control intervention to remove the source of the contamination and prevent further accumulation.

Nesting Materials

Finding evidence of nesting materials, such as twigs, leaves, shredded paper, or insulation fibers, in areas like your attic, garage, or shed, indicates that an animal is preparing to or has already given birth or is seeking shelter. Animals choose locations that are safe, warm, and hidden to raise their young.

Why it Matters:

Finding nesting materials means you likely have a transient or resident animal that is making itself comfortable. This can lead to more significant problems as the animals establish themselves and potentially reproduce within your home. Removing nests and the animals associated with them requires careful handling, especially if young are present, to ensure the humane removal of the entire family unit. This is where professional wildlife control expertise is particularly valuable, as we understand the best methods for humane removal of families. We offer bee and wasp removal separately, but it's part of the overall wildlife conversation.

Burrows or Tunnels on Your Property

Discovering holes or tunnels dug into your lawn, garden, or under structures like decks or sheds can be frustrating examples of wildlife activity. Animals like groundhogs, moles, or voles create these underground networks for shelter, foraging, or travel.

Why it Matters:

Burrows and tunnels can damage the aesthetics of your landscaping and create tripping hazards. In some cases, extensive tunneling can even compromise the stability of foundations or other structures. Identifying the animal responsible and implementing appropriate control methods, such as trapping and relocation, is essential to prevent further damage and safely remove the burrowing animal. Our wildlife control services include addressing these ground-level nuisances safely and effectively. Speaking of ground level, ant control is also a common request for Broomfield homeowners.

Property Damage Beyond the Entry Point

Wildlife can cause damage beyond just the initial entry points. This can include torn screens on windows or doors, damaged siding from climbing animals, or even structural damage to outbuildings. The extent of the damage often depends on the type and size of the animal, as well as how long they have been present.

Why it Matters:

Ignoring property damage caused by wildlife can lead to more significant issues down the line. Damaged screens and siding create additional entry points for other pests and can expose your home's structure to the elements, leading to further deterioration. Addressing both the present wildlife issue and repairing the damage they've caused is crucial for the long-term health and safety of your home.
